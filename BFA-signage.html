BFA Show Signage





Every year, The Institute of Art and Design at New England College hosts the BFA gallery show to celebrate the graduating seniors. Every senior within any BFA concentration is tasked with putting together their Senior Thesis project with the end goal of displaying it on the gallery wall to conclude their time spent at NEC. The opening for the annual show is the friday before seniors graduate, allowing friends, family, and others within the community to gather together in viewing the work these students put into their senior theses, and celebrating their completetion of college.

However, with artwork being displayed in two separate buildings located at The Institute in downtown Manchester, their was a need to guide attendees to and from each building who may not be familiar with the area. For this, the gallery staff at NEC was looking for dynamic, yet legible signage to direct viewers throughout their campus. Using vivid colors with bold text, Hannah crafted these to draw attention to important information while feeling modern and artistic. Several examples of the signs are seen below.





































In addition to the outdoor signs seen above, Hannah also created signage to direct around the inside of the French Hall gallery on 148 Concord St. These signs were much smaller in scale, but used the same theme of dynamic type and bold color to draw attention to viewers while adding visual interest.
